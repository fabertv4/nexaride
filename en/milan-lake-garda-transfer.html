Milan to Lake Garda by train — and where it falls short

Let's be honest: the train is genuinely good on this route. Frecciarossa and Italo high-speed services run from Milano Centrale to Desenzano del Garda-Sirmione in about 50 minutes and to Peschiera del Garda in about 56 minutes, with tickets from €8.90–9.90 if you book ahead; counting every operator there are roughly 35 trains a day to Desenzano and around 26 to Peschiera. Slower Trenord regionals take about 83 and 92 minutes (€10.00 and €11.50), roughly hourly.

The catch is that the stations are not the lake. Sirmione's old town sits about 10 km from Desenzano station — a bus or local taxi ride away — and the western shore (Salò, Gardone Riviera, Gargnano) has no station of its own: from the railway you continue by road. With suitcases, children or an early flight home, every change costs more than it looks.

A private transfer covers the whole journey door-to-door in about an hour and a half: one car from your Milan hotel or the airport straight to your hotel on either shore, your chauffeur handles the bags, and the price is a fixed quote agreed in advance on WhatsApp. It works in both directions — Milan to Lake Garda, or Lake Garda back to Milan and the airports.

Where we take you on Lake Garda

The lake has two shores — Lombard to the west, Veneto to the east — and one transfer from Milan serves both:

  • 🏰 Sirmione — the Rocca Scaligera, a 13th-century moated castle with drawbridge, the Grotte di Catullo (the monumental remains of a 1st-century Roman villa on the tip of the peninsula) and the Aquaria Thermal SPA on the lakefront
  • ⛵ Desenzano del Garda — the lively hub of the lower lake, often called the lake's capital, with the main railway station on the Milan–Venice line
  • 🏛️ Peschiera del Garda — a fortified town on the Mincio whose pentagonal Venetian walls have been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2017
  • 🌴 Salò and Gardone Riviera — the elegant western riviera; Gardone is home to the Vittoriale degli Italiani, Gabriele d'Annunzio's hillside estate-museum with the ship Puglia set into the hill
  • 🍷 Bardolino and Lazise — on the Veneto shore: Bardolino is the village behind the DOC wine and its vineyard hills; Lazise is one of the few Garda towns with its medieval walls fully preserved, watched over by the Scaliger castle

Gardaland with kids: door to door, no changes

Gardaland, at Castelnuovo del Garda on the Veneto shore, is Italy's most visited theme park — around 3 million visitors a year. The 2026 season runs from 28 March to 1 November, plus Halloween weekends and Magic Winter dates. From Milan it's about 138–145 km, roughly 1h30 by car (longer on summer weekends); the A4 exit is Peschiera del Garda and the park sits just a few kilometres from the motorway.

By train you reach Peschiera del Garda station, about 2 km from the park, then take Gardaland's free shuttle, every 30 minutes on park opening days. It works — it just isn't door-to-door. With a private transfer the chauffeur collects the whole family at your Milan hotel, or at Malpensa, Linate or Bergamo arrivals, and drops you at the park: child seats on request, pushchairs and bags in the boot.

FAQ

How far is Lake Garda from Milan?+
Sirmione is about 132–135 km from Milan via the A4 motorway — roughly 1h20–1h40 by car without traffic. Desenzano del Garda is about 127–128 km (around 1h20–1h30), Peschiera del Garda about 136 km (around 1h25–1h30) and Salò on the western shore about 1h30–1h40.
Is there a train from Milan to Lake Garda?+
Yes, and it's a fair option for the southern shore: high-speed trains from Milano Centrale reach Desenzano del Garda-Sirmione in about 50 minutes and Peschiera del Garda in about 56 minutes, with tickets from €8.90 booked ahead; Trenord regionals take about 83 and 92 minutes. The stations, however, are not the lake: Sirmione's old town is still some 10 km from Desenzano station, and west-shore towns like Salò or Gardone Riviera have no station of their own — a private transfer takes you door-to-door.
Can you pick us up at Bergamo or Malpensa airport?+
Yes. Bergamo Orio al Serio (BGY) is the closest airport to the lake: about 85 km from Sirmione, around 55 minutes by car (allow 1h–1h15 in summer), while the bus alternative takes about 2 hours to Desenzano plus a local connection. We also collect from Malpensa and Linate, with flight monitoring and Meet & Greet in the arrivals hall.
Which towns on Lake Garda do you serve?+
Both shores. On the Lombard side: Sirmione, Desenzano del Garda, Salò, Gardone Riviera and Gargnano; on the Veneto side: Peschiera del Garda, Lazise, Bardolino and Castelnuovo del Garda for Gardaland. We also cover return journeys — from Lake Garda back to Milan or straight to the airports.
How much does a Milan–Lake Garda transfer cost?+

Is the transfer a good option for families going to Gardaland?+
Very much so. Gardaland, at Castelnuovo del Garda, is Italy's most visited theme park (around 3 million visitors a year) and the 2026 season runs from 28 March to 1 November. We drop you at the park — no train-plus-shuttle changes — with child seats on request and room for the whole family in the V-Class.
