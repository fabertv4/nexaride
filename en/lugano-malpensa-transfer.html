Distances, times and the border crossing

From Lugano the route follows the Swiss A2 and crosses into Italy at Gaggiolo (Stabio–Cantello), joining the A8 towards Varese and Gallarate; the alternative runs via Chiasso-Brogeda onto the A9. Gaggiolo is open to travellers 24 hours a day, and your driver picks the better crossing on the day. Swiss motorways require the CHF 40 vignette, sold at the Gaggiolo-Stabio, Ponte Tresa and Chiasso crossings — with a chauffeur, none of that is your problem.

Train, bus or private transfer between Lugano and Malpensa?

The honest answer first: there is a direct train. The cross-border TILO S50 line (Biasca–Bellinzona–Lugano–Mendrisio–Varese–Malpensa) stops at both Malpensa T1 and T2, takes about 1h41 from Lugano to Terminal 2 and costs €21 in 2nd class (€27 in 1st). It runs hourly, calling at all regional stations; from Malpensa T2 there are services from 06:13 to 23:13. Good value — if your flight times match the timetable. One caveat for summer 2026: TILO has announced that from 7 June to 5 July the S50 and S40 are cancelled between Stabio, Varese and Malpensa Airport, so the direct link is suspended during that period.

By road there is FlixBus, with an average journey of 1h30, fares from €14.98 and 3 departures a day (first 04:55, last 20:55), plus the airport shuttles listed by Lugano's official tourism site — Malpensa-express (Giosy Tours), Busnavetta and BUXSI — on the Malpensa–Chiasso/Mendrisio/Lugano corridor.

A private transfer covers the same route door-to-door in roughly 60–75 minutes, at whatever hour your flight requires: one car from your Lugano address straight to the terminal (or the reverse), flight tracking on arrivals, and a fixed quote agreed in advance on WhatsApp — no timetable, no platform changes with luggage, no shuttle stops along the way.

Where we pick up and drop off in Lugano

Lugano is Switzerland's third financial centre and a conference, banking and business hub, set on a bay of the north shore of Lake Lugano. We collect from hotels, offices and private addresses across the city — including during the LongLake Festival, the open-air event the City of Lugano stages every July (16th edition: 9–26 July 2026):

  • 🏛️ Villa Principe Leopoldo — boutique 5-star on the Collina d'Oro with lake views, 37 suites, former summer residence of Prince Friedrich Leopold of Prussia

  • 🏛️ Banks, offices and conference venues — recurring pickups for corporate clients, with invoice
  • 🏛️ Private addresses in Lugano and across Canton Ticino, on request

FAQ

How long does the transfer from Lugano to Malpensa take?+
It's about 78–80 km by road: roughly 60–65 minutes without traffic, more realistically 60–75 minutes allowing for the border crossing. From Lugano the route follows the Swiss A2 and crosses into Italy at Gaggiolo (Stabio–Cantello), joining the A8 towards Varese and Gallarate; the alternative runs via Chiasso onto the A9. Your driver picks the better crossing on the day.
Is the TILO train or a private transfer better between Lugano and Malpensa?+
The TILO S50 cross-border line links Lugano directly with both Malpensa terminals: about 1h41 to Terminal 2, €21 in 2nd class (€27 in 1st), running hourly and calling at all regional stations. It's good value if your timings match the timetable. A private transfer covers the same route door-to-door in roughly 60–75 minutes, at any hour, with the car waiting if your flight is late.
Can you pick us up for an early-morning flight or a late-night arrival?+
Yes, we operate 24/7. For departures we collect you from any Lugano address at whatever hour your check-in requires; for arrivals we monitor your flight and the driver waits in the arrivals hall with a name sign — Meet & Greet included — even after midnight.
What documents do we need for the border crossing?+
Switzerland is in the Schengen area but outside the EU customs union: keep a valid identity document or passport with you, as random customs checks are possible at the border. Your chauffeur handles the route through Gaggiolo or Chiasso-Brogeda — the Gaggiolo crossing is open to travellers 24 hours a day.
Can we get an invoice or set up recurring corporate transfers?+
Yes. Lugano is Switzerland's third financial centre and many of our clients on this route are business travellers: we issue invoices and can agree standing arrangements for recurring pickups — same driver, same billing, one WhatsApp thread.
