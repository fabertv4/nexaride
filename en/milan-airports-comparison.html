Milan Airports Compared: Malpensa, Linate or Bergamo Orio al Serio?

Milan is served by 3 different airports, each with its own characteristics, distances and flight types. This guide helps you choose the right one for your trip and understand the best way to reach the city.

Quick comparison table

Airport IATA Code Distance to Milan center Typical driving time Flights operated
Milan Malpensa MXP ~50 km 50-70 min Long-haul, international, main hub
Milan Linate LIN ~7 km 15-25 min Domestic, short-haul European, business
Bergamo Orio al Serio BGY ~50 km 50-60 min Low-cost (Ryanair, Wizz Air), charter

Times indicative under normal traffic, from/to Milan center. In rush hours can increase by 30-50%.

Which airport should you choose?

Malpensa (MXP)

Best for intercontinental and long-haul flights, traditional carriers (ITA Airways, Lufthansa, Emirates, etc.). Milan's reference airport.

Linate (LIN)

Best for same-day business trips and domestic flights. Proximity to the center (7 km) makes it unbeatable in terms of time.

Orio al Serio (BGY)

Best for low-cost travellers (Ryanair, Wizz Air). Although in Bergamo, it serves the Milan metropolitan area and is well connected.

FAQ

Which is the main airport of Milan?

Milan-Malpensa (MXP) is Milan's main and largest airport, the hub for long-haul and intercontinental flights. Linate (LIN) is the city airport for domestic and short-haul European routes. Bergamo Orio al Serio (BGY), although 45 km from Milan, serves the metropolitan area as a low-cost hub.

How far is Malpensa from Milan center?

About 50 km. Typical driving time: 50-70 minutes depending on traffic and time of day.

How far is Linate from Milan center?

About 7 km. It is the city airport: by car it takes 15-25 minutes.

How far is Orio al Serio from Milan?

About 45-50 km. Typical driving time: 50-60 minutes via A4 motorway.

Which airport should I choose for a business trip?

Linate for same-day trips thanks to its proximity to the center. Malpensa if the flight is international. Orio al Serio is competitive for low-cost flights.

Is private transfer better than public transport?

Private NCC transfer is convenient for groups (2+ passengers), late-night flights with limited train/bus availability, bulky luggage, or when you want to arrive directly at the address without changes.
